About Summer

Summer was born in August 2014 and brought constant joy, silliness, and positivity to our family. She was an easy-going girl who loved all things Disney, especially Pixar’s Monsters, Inc., and taking excellent care of her three dogs: Daisy, Buzz, and Lilly. She had a passion for music, sparkly manicures, and a constant stream of Mickey Mouse and Doc McStuffins.

In March 2015, at just seven months old, doctors discovered a 10 cm mass in her liver. What was initially thought to be hepatoblastoma was later identified as a rare Malignant Rhabdoid Tumor. For over a year and a half, Summer bravely faced continuous, high-intensity treatments, including extensive surgeries, a stem cell transplant, and radiation.

[span_7](start_span)She never let her cancer define her, and even on the hardest days, she found a way to play and be happy[span_7](end_span). [span_8](start_span)She passed away at home, surrounded by our family, in September 2016 at the age of two[span_8](end_span). [span_9](start_span)She was the strongest person any of us will ever get the chance to meet, and she remains my absolute hero[span_9](end_span).

The Summer Strong Hero Fund

Shortly after Summer’s diagnosis, our family and friends became involved with Alex's Lemonade Stand Foundation to try and make lemonade out of an unimaginable situation. [span_10](start_span)The world of childhood cancer is scary and uncontrollable, but focusing on making a positive impact helped us navigate the toughest times[span_10](end_span).

[span_11](start_span)Today, Team Summer Strong has grown into an incredible community with supporters ranging from age 1 to 95[span_11](end_span). [span_12](start_span)We have been represented across the country at lemonade stands, school fundraisers, and races in Orlando, New York, and Philadelphia[span_12](end_span).
